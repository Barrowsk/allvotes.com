Dates and Deadlines

Voter Registration Deadlines

21 days before Election Day. Election Day registration is available at a town office or city hall only.

The 2020 Election

  • Presidential Primary Elections: March 3
  • Partisan Primary (other offices) Registration Deadline: May 22
  • Partisan Primary (other offices): June 9
  • General Election Registration Deadline: October 13
  • General Election: November 3

Register at Home or School

Students have two options about where to register to vote.

Students may choose to register at their campus address or at their permanent or home address. You may only be registered and vote in one location.

What type of ID do I need?

To Register

The registration form requires you to put down your Maine driver’s license or state ID card number, or if you don’t have one of those, then the last 4 digits of your Social Security number. If the state is able to verify that information, then you will not need to show any other identification.

If you are registering for the first time and register by mail or voter registration drive, and those numbers cannot be verified, or if you are registering at the polls on Election Day, you will need to present one of the following forms of identification:

  • A government issued photo ID, including a U.S. Passport, military ID, driver's license or state ID
  • Other government-issued ID without a photograph such as a certified copy of a birth certificate or a signed Social Security card
  • A utility bill, bank statement, a government paycheck, a paycheck, a document confirming eligibility for public benefits, or other government document with the voter’s name and address

To Vote

You do not have to show or submit an ID to vote.

How can I vote?

By Mail

Any registered voter may cast an absentee ballot without an excuse. Contact your local election officials for a mail-in ballot. Find information here. You can request an absentee ballot electronically here.

Absentee ballots may be requested beginning three months before Election Day and up to three business days before Election Day. Absentee ballots must be received by the municipal clerk by 8 p.m. on Election Day.

Early In-Person

Maine voters can vote early by filling out an absentee ballot in person at the county clerk’s office prior to Election Day. Absentee ballots are generally available at a municipal clerk’s office 30-45 days before Election Day. You can register in person and vote early on the same day.

On Election day

Voting sites will open between 6 a.m. and 10 a.m., depending on your municipality, and will close at 8 p.m. on Election Day. If you are in line by the closing time then you must be allowed to vote. You can find information about your voting site and hours here.
