Register at Home or School

Students have two options about where to register to vote.

Students may choose to register at their campus address or at their permanent or home address. You may only be registered and vote in one location.

What type of ID do I need?

To Register

Alaska’s voter registration form asks for your Alaska driver’s license or ID number, your full Social Security Number, or the last four digits of your Social Security Number.

If you are a first-time registrant and intend to vote by mail in your first election, you must also submit a copy of a valid ID with your application. See below for examples of acceptable ID.

To Vote

A wide range of IDs are acceptable such as:

  • A state issued driver's license or ID card
  • An official voter ID card
  • A U.S. Passport
  • A hunting or fishing license issued by Alaska
  • Other current photo ID
  • Non-photo ID that includes the voter’s name and address like a utility bill, bank statement, government check, or other government issued documents are also acceptable

Voters who cannot present an ID may still vote if they are positively identified by an election official, or by casting a provisional ballot. Provisional ballots are counted 15 days after an election, if voting officials can verify that a voter is qualified to vote.

How can I vote?

By Mail

Any registered Alaska voter may apply to receive an absentee ballot my mail. Contact your local election officials for an absentee ballot application or access the application online here.

Mailed absentee ballot applications must be received no later than 10 days before the election. But, if you are registering to vote, or updating your address, name, or party affiliation and requesting an absentee ballot your absentee application must be postmarked at least 30 days prior to the election. Returned absentee ballots must be postmarked on or before Election Day.

Your returned absentee ballot must be accompanied by a notarized affidavit (which comes with the ballot). If you’re unable to get a notary, you may get the affidavit signed by one witness 18 years or older.

Early In-Person

Available at specific voting locations beginning 15 days before Election Day. Early voting dates, times, and locations can be found here.

On Election day

Voting sites will be open from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. on Election Day. If you are in line by closing time, you have the right to cast a ballot. You can find more information about your voting site and hours here.

By Electronic Transmission

Voters may request to receive a ballot by fax or electronic transmission for primary, general, and statewide special elections. You may apply for an electronic transmission ballot until 5 p.m. the day before the election. Electronically received ballots must be signed by a witness aged 18 years or older or an authorized official like a notary public.

You can return your completed ballot by mail or fax. Mailed ballots must be postmarked before or on election day. Faxed ballots must be received by 8 p.m. on election day. More information can be found here.
